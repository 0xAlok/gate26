Spaced Repetition Guide
1. Initial Learning (Day 0)
Attend the lecture, mark the topic as Done in the Syllabus tab, and log the topic in the "Topics Studied" bar. Your homework tonight isn't to reread notes; practice 10 basic PYQs or DPPs to cement the concepts immediately.
2. First Revision (Within 48h)
Do a quick review of your class notes. Go to the Syllabus Tracker and click the rev checkbox. The Self-Assessment modal will classify your confidence (Weak, Shaky, Confident) and automatically schedule your next review date (e.g., 3 days for Shaky).
3. The Daily Routine (Active Recall)
Every day, check the Due Review banner. When a topic pops up, revise it using Active Recall without reading your notes. Take a blank paper and explicitly write out the formulas, conceptual rules (like FCFS scheduling), or execute a Gantt chart. Then, attempt a PYQ and hit rev again. Your interval will expand dynamically (e.g., 7 days → 14 days → 30 days).
4. Strategic Testing
Take Topic-Wise Tests after your first or second successful spaced repetition cycle (around the 7-day mark) when concepts have settled. Give Subject-Wise Tests ONLY when the entire subject shows 0 WEAK topics in the syllabus filter. Log these scores in the Insights panel.
